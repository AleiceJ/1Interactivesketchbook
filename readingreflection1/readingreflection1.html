Affordances & Signifiers

When thinking of affordances in my microproject, I have to keep saying "afford, is for" to help make sense of it, as the author described it in that way. I see the main affordance is pressing keys to interact with the project. The system also is for watching visual changes on the screen that is feedback from pressing the keys. These affordances are signified through text, where at the tops it says to " click the correct letter to complete the rainbow". Looking back at this, it is not as clear as I understand it. The user could think to click on the letters on the screen, where I meant for them to use their keyboard to press the letters. I think that it is more so implied that the letters that are on the screen are the letters that are only allowed to be used. Those letters are the signifiers.

Feedback

The system provides feedback when a key is pressed. If the correct key is pressed, a new color section appears this feedback is immediate, so the user knows the did something correct. It could also be considered not fully sufficient because their is no immediate feedback for wrong keys pressed, after 2 the rainbow starts over, but nothing happens after 1 wrong key. Per the reading, the author would criticize this because he believes feedback should clearly communicate what happened and what to do next

Mapping

The mapping between input and outcome is easily understandable in my opinion. You press a key and the rainbow grows. If you don't press the correct key, it does not grow. I can aslo say that there is no visible connection between the letters and colors. The functions are not clearly stated, if the user wanted to know why a letter gave say a blue, there would be no clear answet or reasoning, it just be because it does. That is not clear design. There is information missing, or functions that are not clearly defined that cause confusion.

Constraints

The constraints of the system are that only specific letters work and that there are a limited amount of steps. These constraints are slightly invisible because users are not told which letters work, it is left for them to infer that the letters on the screen are the ones that are to be used. The constraints of my microproject are set to guide the user, but also have the potential to frustrate the user because they are not told and have to guess.
Biggest Design Weakness
After the reading, I can see that the biggest design weakness is the lack of clear signifiers and feedback. According to Norman, good design should clearly communicate what is possible and what just happened. In my microproject, the users must guess the correct input and the system does not explain mistakes, this makes the interaction less human centered. Without clear signifiers and feedback, it makes the project or the logic of the project harder to learn.
Biggest Takeaway from the Text
My biggest takeaway is that design should match how people think and behave. Also that as a designer, it is my job to make things clear and easily understandable. I can not expect people to infer or behave in the way I would, because I am not desigining for me. Norman emphasizes that design should not force users to figure things out, but instead clearly guide them. Human centered design is created for the people, based off how they act and it encourages good behavior, while bad design creates confusion and errors. This reading has also made me look at my design differently, in my microproject, there were opportunities where I could have made things clearer to better guide the user through the project. The use of everyday problems in his reference to good and bad design helped me to understand how things we might see as simple and easy to understand are not always easily understood by others.
